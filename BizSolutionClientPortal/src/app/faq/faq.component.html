2. Can you give an example for TDS on property compliance for better understanding?

Ans: You can refer the below details on section 194IA.

Scope of section 194IA:

Section 194IA, provides that any person, being a transferee, responsible for paying to a resident transferor any sum by way of consideration for transfer of any immovable property (other than agricultural land) shall deduct an amount equal to one per cent of such sum as Income-tax at the time of credit of such sum to the account of the transferor or at the time of payment of such sum in cash or by issue of cheque or draft or by any other mode, whichever is earlier towards TDS on purchase of property.

Who is the payer?

Ans: The payer is any person, being a transferee, responsible for paying to a resident transferor any sum by way of consideration for transfer of any immovable property.

Who is the payee?

Ans: The payee is resident transferor of any immovable property.

28. What is section 194 IA of income tax act?

Ans: Section 194IA of the Income Tax Act mandates that a buyer of a property with a value that exceeds Rs 50 Lakhs must pay TDS on Purchase of property of 1 % of the property value when settling the amount to the seller.

29. What is the TDS on purchase of property by an NRI?

Ans: The rate of TDS on purchase of property by an NRI is the same as that of a non NRI. The standard 1 % of the property value as stated in Section 194IA of Income Tax Act.

30. What is the TDS on sale of property online payment?

Ans: The TDS on sale of property must be paid using Form 26QB within 30 days from the end of the month paid by the buyer as per section 194IA of the Income tax act. After depositing TDS to the government, the buyer is required to furnish the TDS certificate Form 16B to the seller. This is available around 4-5 days after depositing the TDS for download in Traces website. The Form 16B Certificate is mandatory for registration of property.

31. What is TDS on sale of property 26QB?

Within 30 days after the end of the month in which the transaction occurred, Form 26QB must be filled and the TDS on sale of property of 1 % has to be paid as per Section 194IA of the Income tax act, Form 16B is generated in Traces website and submitted towards registration of property.

32. What is 26QB TDS?

Form 26QB is a form filled by the purchasers to submit their Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)  for the transaction of a property purchase.

The Due date of filing Form 26QB is within 30 days from the end of month in which the tax deduction is made. For example, Mr. Ram has paid the amount to seller on any date in the month of April 2024 for purchase of immovable property, he needs to fill form 26QB on or before 30th May 2024 and make the TDS payment.

33. What is 194 IATDS rate?

The 194IA TDS rate is 1 % of the property total consideration value during sale.

34. What is section 194ia of income tax act?

Section 194IA of income tax act mandates that the buyer of an immovable property must pay a TDS on purchase of property of 1 % while making the payment to the owner. . The TDS Tax is only applicable for properties with  total consideration value of over Rs 50 Lakhs.

35. What is the online payment of TDS on sale of property?

TDS on purchase of property or TDS on sale of property must be paid by the buyer of the property as per section 194IA of income tax act. The TDS payable  is 1 % of the total consideration value by filling the Form 26QB. This payment must be made towards TDS during each payment to the owner of the property. On Payment of the TDS on property, Form 16B is generated in Traces website and is required to be submitted to the seller for registration.
